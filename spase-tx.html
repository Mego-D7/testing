explain

The solar system consists of eight planets, including Earth, that orbit around the Sun. The Sun is a dynamic and active star, with various phenomena occurring on its surface, such as solar flares and coronal mass ejections (CMEs). Solar flares and CMEs can release a huge amount of energy, including X-rays and ultraviolet radiation, which can interact with Earth's magnetic field and atmosphere. Geomagnetic storms can cause spectacular aurora displays, but they can also disrupt radio communications, navigation systems, and power grids. The Errera effect is a disturbance of the Earth's magnetic field that can cause errors in navigation systems, such as GPS, and disrupt communication systems.
